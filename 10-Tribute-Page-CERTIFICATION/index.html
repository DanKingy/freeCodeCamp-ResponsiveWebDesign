Alan Mathison Turing

The father of modern Computer Science

Timeline of Alan Turing's life

  • 1912 - born in London, England
  • 1928 - Turing first encountered Albert Einstein's work
  • 1930 - Turing's first love, Christopher Collan Morcom, died from complications of bovine tuberculosis. He wrote to Morcom's mother:
    I am sure I could not have found anywhere another companion so brilliant and yet so charming and unconceited. I regarded my interest in my work, and in such things as astronomy (to which he introduced me) as something to be shared with him and I think he felt a little the same about me ... I know I must put as much energy if not as much interest into my work as if he were alive, because that is what he would like me to do
  • 1934 - Graduates from King's College with a first-class honours in Mathematics
  • 1936 - Publishes his paper "On Computable Numbers, with an Application to the Entscheidungsproblem". The paper proved that a hypothetical machine, the Turing machine, is capable of performing any conceivable mathematical computation if it was representable as an algorithm. He then went on to prove that there was no solution to the decision problem.
  • 1936 - He spent the next couple of years studying under Alonzo Church, both coming together to form the Church-Turing thesis
  • 1938 - He worked with the Government Code and Cypher School (GC&CS), the British codebreaking organisation
  • 1939 - Turing reported to Bletchley Park, the wartime station of GC&CS
  • 1940 - The first bombe machine was installed in Bletchley Park. Although Polish mathematicians had broke the Enigma, this machine could do it faster and more effectively. It is estimated his work shortened the war in Europe by more than two years and saved over 14 million lives
  • 1941 - Turing proposed marriage to Hut 8 colleague Joan Clarke, a fellow mathematician and cryptanalyst, but their engagement was short-lived. After admitting his homosexuality to his fiancee, Turing decided he could not go through with the marriage
  • 1952 - Homosexual acts were criminal offences in the UK and Turing was convicted of gross indency. His probation was conditional on his agreement to undergo chemical castration, which rendered Turing impotent and caused breast tissue to form. His conviction also led to the removal of his security clearance and barred him from continuing with his cryptographic consultancy, he kept his academic job however.
  • 1954 - Turing died from suspected suicide as a result of cyanide poisoning
